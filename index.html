Protect the watersheds in your area

Watersheds are areas of land that channel water into rivers and creeks. Keeping these areas clean will help maintain healthy watersheds.

Water your lawn sparingly at certain hours

If you water during the day when the sun is out, the water will evaporate much more quickly. Instead, water during the morning or evening. Lawns also don't need to be watered every day. Watering once every three days is enough to keep your lawn healthy.

Pay attention to leaks in your home

All sorts of appliances in your home can leak. Some include faucets, shower heads, and dishwashers. Make sure to inform someone whenever you find a leak to get it fixed.

Use a dedicated bottle or cup for water

Refill the bottle or cup you're using instead of getting a new one. This cuts back on the number of dishes that need to get washed.

Take shorter showers

Every minute trimmed from a shower will save 3 to 6 gallons of water, so try to keep your shower times as short as possible!

Put a nozzle on garden hoses

Left unattended, a running garden hose can waste over 100 gallons of water in just minutes. Put a nozzle on your hose so you only use what you need when you're watering the plants, cleaning the house, or washing the car.

Don't let the faucet run too long

When you're in the bathroom, turn off the tap while brushing your teeth. In the kitchen, use a dishpan to hold soapy water and then rinse dishes with clean water quickly.

Never use the toilet to throw away trash

Throwing trash in the toilet can use a lot of unnecessary water with each flush. It also has the potential to clog up your plumbing which is expensive to fix.
